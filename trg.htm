"Theory Reading Group" at Dartmouth Algorithms & TCS Group


We have an active student-led ``reading group'' where we have active, exciting discussions on recent research in TCS, both internal and external. Some recent and upcoming talks are mentioned below.

  • (2023-12-15) Jonathan talked about his recent work resolving the Steiner Point Removal problem in minor-free graphs.
  • (2023-12-8) Manuel talked about solving the Tree Evaluation problem in O(log n log log n) space.
  • (2023-11-17) Tamalika Mukherjee from Columbia talked about How to Make Your Approximation Algorithm Private.
  • (2023-11-10) Jessica Sorrell from UPenn talked about connections between replicability, privacy, and perfect generalization.
  • (2023-11-3) Jamie Schmidt from Dartmouth Math talked about his recent work on the Erdős vs. Szekeres combinatorial game.
  • (2023-10-27) Vignesh Viswanath from UMass Amherst talked about fair allocation with simple submodular valuations.
  • (2023-10-20) Ankita talked about her recent work on fault-tolerant k-supplier with outliers.
  • (2023-10-13) Jamie Schmidt from Dartmouth Math talked about a book proof of the middle levels theorem.
  • (2023-10-12) Sruthi Gorantla from IISc Bangalore talked about her work on ex-post group fairness and individual fairness in ranking.
  • (2023-10-6) Prantar Ghosh from Rutgers talked about his work on lower bounds in the Merlin-Arthur communication and graph streaming verification.
  • (2023-10-4) Jonathan talked about the equivalence between GapUNSAT data structures and E^NP lower bounds.
  • (2023-9-29) Luc Cote talked about maximizing monotone submodular functions over a matroid.
  • (2023-9-22) Hsien-Chih talked about how to compute planar diameter in subquadratic time.
  • (2023-9-15) Manuel talked about his recent work on the missing item finding problem.
